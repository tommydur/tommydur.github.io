Computing in Medicine

Examples of Computing in Medicine

  • Medical Imaging
  • Computer-Assisted Surgery
  • Medical Research

Medical Imaging

Medical imaging is the use of technology to create images of the human body for study and diagnosis.

Computers are used for controlling scans such as MRI, CT and even X-rays. These scans are often crucial to a patient's recovery and would not be possible without Computing.

Computer-Assisted Surgery

Computers are often used to perform surgery, as well as plan and teach surgery to new surgeons.

One of the largest manufactorers and pioners of robotic surgery is Intuitive Surgical which pioneered robotic surgery through the early 2000s but is now having to compete against companies like Johnson and Johnson

Medical Research

Complex computer simulations are crucial to the majority of modern research performed to learn more about incurable diseases such as cencer and AIDS.

Supercomputers and very large distributed computer systems are used to analyse millions of outcomes and extraordinary volumes of medical data that would be impossible to analyze manually.
